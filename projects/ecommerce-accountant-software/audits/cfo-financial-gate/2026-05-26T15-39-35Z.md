## A. CFO verdict

**FAIL** — score **38 / 100** (1 × P0, 3 × P1, 2 × P2, 1 × P3).

> Scope: customer credits / prepayments / deposits / overpayments / refund-credit, post-GH-182.
> This is run #1 — no prior run to diff against, so no trend / Δ line.

The customer-payment backend is internally balanced (every journal ties debits = credits), but it is **not financially safe to put real customer money through yet**. When a customer pays more than they owe, the excess is booked as a *negative balance inside `1100 Accounts Receivable` (an ASSET)* and is **never reclassified to the `2200 Customer deposits` LIABILITY that already exists in the chart of accounts**. The money the business owes back to the customer is therefore not recognised as a liability, and the reporting layer (`GREATEST(open_receivable, 0)`) clamps the surplus to zero so it is invisible in every customer KPI and the settings-based balance sheet. There is no path to refund a credit balance, no logged accounting decision for any of this, and no test exercising the overpayment case. This is the exact gap `FINANCIAL_LOGIC_BLUEPRINT.md` §7 open question #4 warned about, and the financial-standards Standard 9 names as a P0.

## D. Current customer payment flow

Two entry points create `CustomerPayment` rows, both journal-posted:

1. **`POST /api/customer-payments` → `addCustomerPayment`** (the GH-182 customer-account path):
   - `requireSession({ permission: "receivables:write" })` (route.ts:13) — actor from verified session, not headers ✅ (Standard 3).
   - Single `prisma.$transaction`: `customerPayment.create` → (if `order_id`) recompute `amount_paid`/`amount_due`/`payment_status` → `writeAuditLog` → `postCustomerPaymentJournal` (sales.service.ts:637–689). Write + journal + audit atomic ✅ (Standard 2).
   - Journal: `Dr <cash-by-method> / Cr 1100 AR` for the full amount (posting.service.ts:334–348). Revenue untouched ✅ (Standard for AR-not-revenue).
2. **`handleOrderLifecycleEvent` PAID** and **`createPosCheckout`** post the same `postCustomerPaymentJournal` helper.

The journal helper is sound *for a payment ≤ what is owed*. The defect is entirely in **what happens to the excess** and in **the absence of any deposit/credit concept**.

## E. Current overpayment behaviour

Concrete trace — customer owes **100**, pays **150** via `addCustomerPayment` (no lifecycle guard on this path):

1. `customerPayment.create({ amount: 150 })` — full 150 stored (sales.service.ts:638).
2. If an `order_id` is supplied: `amount_due = max(order_total − 150, 0) = 0` (calculations.ts:42) and `payment_status = PAID`. The 50 surplus **is not represented anywhere on the order**.
3. `postCustomerPaymentJournal({ amount: 150 })` posts **`Dr cash 150 / Cr 1100 AR 150`** (posting.service.ts:334–348).
4. Net effect on `1100 Accounts receivable`: it now carries a **50 credit (negative) balance** — a contra-asset.
5. `2200 Customer deposits` is **untouched** (0).

`addCustomerPayment` accepts the overpayment **silently** (no cap, no warning, no second leg). The sibling lifecycle PAID path (sales.service.ts:722) would have *rejected* the same overpayment with "Payment cannot exceed the amount due." — so the system's behaviour depends on which door the money comes through. The GH-182 door is the permissive one.

For a **payment with no `order_id`** (website/card paid before an order is matched): step 2 is skipped; the journal still posts `Dr cash / Cr 1100` → `1100` goes negative with **no order, no customer-deposit liability, and no UI surface** to see or apply it.

## F. Current accounting / journal treatment

| Concern | Treatment today | Correct treatment |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Excess over `amount_due` | `Cr 1100 AR` (negative asset) | `Cr 2200 Customer deposits` (liability) |
| Pure deposit / prepayment (no order) | `Cr 1100 AR` (floating negative AR) | `Cr 2200` per-customer sub-account |
| Apply credit to a future order | Implicitly: a later fulfilment `Dr 1100` nets the negative AR back up — works **only if** the same order is later fulfilled; never reclassifies through `2200` | `Dr 2200 / Cr 1100` (or settle order) when the credit is consumed |
| Refund a credit | **No path** | `Dr 2200 / Cr cash` — distinct from the `4200` sale-return path |

The ledger **balances** (the entry is `Dr=Cr`), so the trial balance still ties. The corruption is **classification**: liabilities are understated by the surplus, AR is understated (driven negative), and the business's obligation to the customer is invisible on the books.

`2200` is fully wired on the **read** side — `ledger-reporting.service.ts:647` (`liabilityCustomerDeposits`) and `finance.service.ts:450` both surface a "Customer deposits" line — they are simply always 0 because nothing posts to it. The plumbing exists; the posting leg is missing.

## G. Is excess customer money a liability, or lost / hidden?

**Neither destroyed nor a recognised liability — it is mis-classified as a negative asset and then hidden by reporting.**

- **Not destroyed:** the cash debit is recorded; the entry balances; the 50 is recoverable by reading `1100` line detail.
- **Not a liability:** `2200` is never credited, so no report that reads liabilities shows the amount owed back to the customer.
- **Hidden:** `customer_summary.open_receivable = GREATEST(…, 0)` (migration :669) and `computeCustomerReceivable = max(…, 0)` (calculations.ts:42) clamp the surplus to 0. The customer KPI, the receivables roll-up, and the settings-based balance sheet (`receivables = SUM(open_receivable)`, migration :777) all show 0 — while the *ledger* balance sheet reads `1100` directly and shows it **negative** (ledger-reporting.service.ts:637). The two pictures of AR silently disagree.

Net: money the business owes a customer can sit on the books invisibly, under an asset account, with no liability and no way to refund it.

## J. P1 risks

**P1-1 — Reporting floors `open_receivable` at 0, hiding the surplus, and the view-AR vs ledger-AR representations diverge.**
*Standard 8 (source-of-truth). Evidence:* migration `:669` (`GREATEST(…,0)`), `calculations.ts:42` (`max(…,0)`), `ledger-reporting.service.ts:637` (ledger `1100` goes negative). Must be resolved before real data so an operator can see and reconcile money owed back.

**P1-2 — No customer-credit / deposit refund path.**
*Standard 11. Evidence:* grep #5 — only `postReturnRefundJournal` (`4200`, sale returns) exists; no `Dr 2200 / Cr cash`. **P1-3 — The customer-credit accounting policy (§7 #4) is unresolved, yet `addCustomerPayment` implicitly settles it in code — and inconsistently.**
*Standards 17 & 9. Standard 17 makes "resolving an open accounting question without a logged decision" at least P1. The two code paths disagree on whether overpayment is even allowed.

## K. Recommended accounting decision points

These are **policy choices the CFO/operator must make and log in `docs/DECISIONS_LOG.md` (or an AgDR) BEFORE code is wired** — the gate flags the gap; it does not settle it (Standard 17).

1. **Overpayment treatment (§7 #4).** Confirm: any customer payment exceeding `amount_due` credits `2200 Customer deposits` for the excess, not `1100`. Decide the account structure — **recommended in the blueprint:** per-customer `2200-{customer_code}` sub-accounts mirroring the per-partner pattern. *Decision needed: flat `2200` vs per-customer sub-account.*
2. **Standalone prepayment / website-card-before-order (§7 #4 + #1).** Decide how a payment with no matched order is booked (`Cr 2200` to a customer sub-account) and when/how it converts to revenue (ties to open question #1: revenue at order-creation vs fulfilment — itself still unresolved).
3. **Applying credit to a future order.** Decide the trigger and entry: `Dr 2200 / Cr 1100` (settle receivable) at order creation/fulfilment, vs operator-initiated application. Define whether partial application is allowed.
4. **Credit refund routing (§7 #8).** Decide whether a `2200` refund returns via the original payment method or operator choice, and confirm it is `Dr 2200 / Cr cash` — never `4200`.
5. **Overpayment guard policy.** Decide whether overpayment is *allowed and routed to credit* (recommended) or *rejected*, then make `addCustomerPayment` and `handleOrderLifecycleEvent` consistent.

## M. Final CFO recommendation

**Do not onboard real customer money through the customer-payment flow until P0-1 is closed.** The journals balance, the session/audit/atomicity controls are sound, and `2200` is already defined and read by both balance sheets — the system is one posting leg and one logged decision away from being correct. But as it stands, an overpayment, a deposit, or a website/card payment received before order-matching silently lands as a hidden negative receivable with no liability and no way to refund it. On real data that means under-reported liabilities, an unexplained gap between the view and ledger balance sheets, and customer money the operator cannot see or return.

**Path to PASS WITH CONDITIONS:** (1) log the customer-credit accounting decision (§K / issue #1); (2) route overpayment + prepayment excess to `2200` (issue #2, +#3 for sub-accounts via `/migration`); (3) build the credit-refund and apply-credit paths (#4, #5); (4) surface the credit balance and reconcile the two AR views (#6); then re-run `/cfo-financial-gate --scope=customer-credits-prepayments-refunds`. Closing P0-1 alone lifts the verdict off FAIL; closing the P1s clears the conditions.
