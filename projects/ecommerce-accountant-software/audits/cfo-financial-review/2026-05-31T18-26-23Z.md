## A. CFO Executive Summary

The headline finding is the most important one for the founder to internalise:

> **A balanced trial balance does not mean the books are correct.** It only proves that *each posted journal entry* was internally balanced (debits = credits). It says **nothing** about *completeness*. This system has missing and asymmetric postings (the documented Phase-2 wiring gap) and **no opening-balance entry**, so individual account balances are economically nonsensical (negative cash, negative AR/AP) **while the trial balance still balances**. That is exactly the pattern the founder observed.

Three distinct problem classes are in play, and it is important not to conflate them:

1. **Upstream data/posting completeness (the real root cause, not a report bug).** Cash/AR/AP go negative because money-*out* legs are posted while the matching money-*in* legs (owner capital injection, opening cash, the sale-side AR debit, the PO-receipt AP credit) are missing or not yet wired. The balance-sheet/ledger code is computing correctly on bad inputs.

2. **Owner-facing presentation that hides or distorts the above.** The Balance Sheet **balances by construction** via an `unreconciled_adjustment` plug, so its **"Balanced? Yes" indicator is meaningless** and gives false assurance. The Dashboard shows a **clamped, positive-only** receivables figure, so it silently hides the negative-AR signal the Balance Sheet exposes. Same underlying ledger, three different "truths" depending on the page.

3. **Two disconnected sources of truth for owner equity.** Partner deposits/withdrawals/profit-allocations live in an **operational table (`PartnerCapitalTxn`) that is never posted to the ledger.** So the Partners page "capital account" and the Balance Sheet "owner capital/equity" are **independent and will not agree.** Financial Summary then places these operational partner numbers next to ledger numbers without flagging the difference.

**Net:** the calculation engine is mostly sound. The danger is that the current pages present an un-reconciled, partially-posted, demo-grade ledger as if it were finished accounting — and the Owner Dashboard #190 would amplify that to the least technical reader unless the completeness and presentation issues are fixed first. Per Decision 19, accounting was explicitly **not accepted** for go-live; this review is consistent with that.

## B. Page-by-page findings

### 1. `/reports/accounting` (Trial Balance + Journal + Chart of Accounts)
- **Sources:** `posting.service.ensureChartOfAccounts()` (seed-upsert — *writes* missing default accounts on load), `getTrialBalance()`, `listJournalEntries({take:50})`. All ledger (`JournalLine`/`JournalEntry`/`AccountingAccount`).
- **Math:** Correct. `balanced = |ΣDr − ΣCr| < 0.01`. Because every posted entry is internally balanced, this is essentially **always true**.
- **Business meaning:** **This is the trap.** "Balanced? Yes" is read by a non-accountant as "the books are right." It only means "no half-posted single entry." Completeness (are the entries that *should* exist actually there?) is unverified. The page's own **Phase-2 caveat banner** correctly says "treat as validation data, not authoritative" — that banner is doing real work and must not be removed.
- **Side effect:** Visiting the page can create chart-of-accounts rows on a fresh DB (idempotent; no journals). Not a correctness bug, but the page is not purely a report.

### 2. `/reports/balance-sheet`
- **Sources:** `finance.service.getBalanceSheetReport({asOf})` → `getLedgerBalanceSheet()` (ledger, signed `net_movement`) + `readNumericSettings([...])` (settings fallback) + `getArAging()`.
- **Sign convention (intentional, documented):** assets/liabilities use **signed `net_movement`**, *not* the clamped `net_debit`/`net_credit`. So an asset that ends credit-heavy shows **negative** (e.g. cash "overdraft"), and a liability that ends debit-heavy shows negative. This is technically standard accounting ("show the account in its wrong direction, don't clamp"). **But** in this dataset the negatives are not real overdrafts — they are missing-posting artifacts.
- **`unreconciled_adjustment` is a balancing PLUG:** `unreconciled_adjustment = assetsTotal − liabilitiesTotal − baseEquity`; `equityTotal = baseEquity + unreconciled_adjustment`. ⇒ **A = L + E by construction, always.** The page's `balanced` check therefore **cannot be false**. The real diagnostic is the **magnitude** of `unreconciled_adjustment`; the page shows it (warn tone ≥ 0.01) but the footnote "Totals balance by construction" plus a green "Balanced" reads as reassurance, not as a red flag.
- **Settings fallback:** `owner_capital`/`drawings`/`accrued_expenses`/`customer_deposits` fall back to operator settings (or 0) because no posting helper writes 3000/3200 yet. If the operator never set them and never posted an opening balance, owner capital = 0 — guaranteeing the asset/equity mismatch lands in the plug.
- **Inventory:** current on-hand snapshot × weighted-avg cost, even for a past `asOf` (no historical replay — documented). Past-dated balance sheets misstate inventory.

### 3. `/reports/financial-summary`
- **Sources:** `getPnlReport` (ledger), `getBalanceSheetReport` (ledger+settings+plug), `getPartnerCapitalReport` (operational), plus raw `expense`/`purchaseOrder`/`salesOrder` aggregates.
- **Mixing (confirmed):** "Cash In / Investments" comes from **partner deposits (operational `PartnerCapitalTxn`)**; "Profit Available" = **ledger `net_profit` − operational partner `profit_allocations`**. So a single screen blends ledger-backed and operational-only figures, and one tile (Profit Available) is a **subtraction across the two source systems** that are not reconciled to each other. There is no visual marker telling the owner which numbers are audited ledger vs operational scratchpad.
- **No Phase-2 caveat banner** here, even though it surfaces the same partially-posted ledger numbers that Balance Sheet/Accounting flag as non-authoritative.

### 4. `/reports/partners`
- **Sources:** `getPartnerCapitalReport()` over `PartnerCapitalTxn` (operational) + `getPnlReport()` for the allocation *preview*. **Not ledger-posted** — `recordPartnerTxn` inserts a row only; no `JournalEntry`.
- **Negative net contribution (confirmed mechanism):** `net_contribution = deposits − withdrawals`; `capital_account = deposits + profit_allocations − withdrawals − drawings + adjustments`. A partner with a **WITHDRAWAL but zero DEPOSIT/PROFIT_ALLOCATION** yields negative net contribution and negative capital account. The math is correct; the **data is nonsensical** (withdrawing capital never contributed). Nothing validates `capital_account ≥ 0`.
- **Source-of-truth conflict:** because these txns never hit the ledger, the Partners "capital account" and the Balance Sheet "owner capital/equity" are **two independent numbers** that will not match — neither is reconciled to the other.
- **Allocation preview vs committed:** "profit allocated" (committed PROFIT_ALLOCATION txns) sits next to a computed **preview** (`net_profit × ownership%`). Easy to misread the hypothetical as real.

### 5. `/` (Dashboard)
- **Sources:** `getDashboardViewData()` → `getDashboardKpis` (`dashboard_kpis` view + ledger revenue override), `getCustomerReceivablesSummary`, `getLowStockProducts`, `getDashboardDailyCharts`.
- **Hiding mechanism (confirmed):** "Open receivables" reads `dashboard_kpis.open_receivables` and the receivables queue filters `open_receivable > 0` (live-module-data.ts:319) — **customers in credit (negative AR) are excluded**, so the Dashboard shows a clamped positive-only number while the Balance Sheet shows the signed (negative) ledger AR. Two different AR "truths" for the same business.
- **No Phase-2 caveat**, all-time Revenue shown next to a 30-day trend with no window caption.

## D. Root-cause hypotheses (ranked by likelihood)

1. **No opening-balance journal + incomplete/asymmetric postings (Phase-2 wiring gap) on demo data — MOST LIKELY.** Money-out legs exist without money-in legs; no starting cash/owner-capital. Explains negative Cash/AR/AP/Total assets *and* a balanced trial balance simultaneously. Consistent with the Phase-2 caveat banners and Decision 19 ("accounting NOT accepted; runs on demo/staging data"). An opening-balance page exists (`/reports/accounting/opening-balance`) but appears unused.
2. **Deliberate signed-display choice surfaces those artifacts as negatives** (documented in `getLedgerBalanceSheet`). Correct for an accountant, alarming/misleading for an owner. Not a bug per se — a presentation policy that needs owner-facing guarding.
3. **Partner subsystem is operational-only and unvalidated** — withdrawals without deposits permitted; never posted to the ledger; second source of truth for equity. Explains the Partners-page negatives and the Financial-Summary mixing.
4. **Balance-sheet plug (`unreconciled_adjustment`) masks the imbalance** so the only honest signal (a big residual) is dressed as an equity line and overridden by a green "Balanced." Design issue that compounds #1.
5. **Dashboard uses clamped operational summaries** (positive-only AR) instead of signed ledger balances, producing a third inconsistent AR figure. Design issue.
6. **(Lower) Genuine isolated calculation bug in a report.** Not evidenced so far — the math reviewed (P&L, balance sheet, trial balance, partner sums) is internally correct. Keep open pending the browser-evidence pass, but it is not the leading explanation.

## E. P0 / P1 issues that MUST be resolved before Owner Dashboard #190

**P0 — blockers (owner would be actively misled):**
- **P0-1. Trial balance / Balance-sheet "Balanced? Yes" is false assurance.** The plug guarantees balance; the indicator must be reframed (e.g. show `unreconciled_adjustment` as a prominent **reconciliation gap**, and only show "balanced" when the residual is ~0 *without* the plug). #190 will put a balance/health number in front of the owner — must not inherit this.
- **P0-2. Ledger completeness / opening balance.** Resolve the missing money-in legs and post an opening balance so Cash/AR/AP are economically valid. Until then, no owner-facing financial snapshot should present these as final. (This is the Phase-2 close, not #190.)
- **P0-3. Single source of truth for owner equity.** Decide whether partner capital posts to the ledger (3000/3200) or remains operational; today the Partners page and Balance Sheet disagree. #190's partner band and financial band cannot both be "right" while these diverge.

**P1 — must fix or explicitly guard before #190 ships:**
- **P1-1. Negative AR/AP reclassification policy.** Define when a credit AR balance reclassifies to 2200 (customer deposit) and a debit AP balance to 1300 (supplier deposit) vs. displays negative. (Decision 22 already set the customer-credit direction — extend/enforce.)
- **P1-2. Consistent AR across Dashboard and Balance Sheet.** Pick one definition (gross open AR vs net-of-credit) and use it everywhere; stop the Dashboard from silently clamping.
- **P1-3. Financial Summary must mark ledger vs operational** figures and not blend them in a single number without a basis note.
- **P1-4. Partner withdrawal guard.** Prevent (or visibly flag) withdrawals exceeding contributed+allocated capital.
